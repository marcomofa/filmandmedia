Explore Our Classes

Industry professionals provide classroom instruction and hands-on training using industry-grade equipment.

Camera & Lighting

Learn camera operation, tripods, framing, exposure, 3-point lighting, and production-set lighting basics.

Post-Production & Editing

Build editing skills using tools like Adobe Premiere, Photoshop, Illustrator, titles, effects, and project organization.

Audio Design

Learn recording, sound editing, mixing, ADR, sound effects, Foley, and how to gather clear audio on set.

Acting & Directing

Explore acting fundamentals, camera performance, directing, set communication, and working with cast and crew.

Screenwriting

Learn script formatting, character development, plot, peer review, collaboration, and confidence-building exercises.

Art & Production Design

Explore props, costumes, sets, makeup, color, storyboards, and visual design for film productions.

What Comes Next?

Employment Opportunities

After completing the program, students may choose to explore employment opportunities by transitioning to Options For All Employment Services.

Alumni Group

Students can stay connected with fellow filmmakers and continue practicing their skills through the Film & Media Alumni Group.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Adults, 18 and over, with an intellectual and/or developmental disability.
  • Interest in film and media production.
  • Regional Center or private funding.
  • Ability to travel independently to and from the production location.
  • Commitment to completing the program and maintaining attendance.
